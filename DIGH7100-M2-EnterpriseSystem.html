Component Description
A longitudinal electronic record of patient health information produced by encounters in one or more care settings. Electronic Health Record (EHR)
A client's healthcare record used by their healthcare provider, often within their specific practice or program. Electronic Medical Record (EMR)
Electronic version for physician/practitioner patient orders, e.g., diagnostics, medications, or patient-care orders. Computerized Provider Order Entry (CPOE) / Provider Order Management (POM)
An electronic version of the medication administration record; eliminates the need for medication cards and medication Kardex processes. Electronic Medication Administration Record (eMAR)
This component is often used with CPOE and eMAR to complete the cycle of patient medication administration safety. Bar Code Medication Administration (BCMA)
This may include patient care assessments and planning along with documentation. It can be nursing specific or be set up as a multi-disciplinary care and documentation system. Patient Care System (PCS)
Systems set up for specific departmental use in areas such as Pharmacy, Laboratory, or Medical Imaging, etc. Ancillary or Diagnostic Systems
Database of all persons within a health information system; usually includes demographic information along with a unique record identifier. Electronic Master Person Index (eMPI)
Components such as health records, abstracting, billing/account receivable, cost accounting or general ledger, etc. Administrative Systems
